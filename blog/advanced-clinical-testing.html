Synthetic Pain Killers

    Synthetic painkillers, also known as synthetic analgesics, are chemically engineered compounds designed to alleviate pain. Unlike natural analgesics derived from plants (e.g., morphine from opium), synthetic painkillers are created through organic synthesis in pharmaceutical laboratories. These compounds play a crucial role in modern medicine, managing acute and chronic pain across a variety of clinical conditions.

Types of Synthetic Pain killers

    1. Non - opioid analgesics:

    Examples: Acetaminophen like paracetamol

    NSAIDs like ibuprofen, diclofenac etc.,

    2. Opioid Analgesics (Synthetic):

    Examples: Fentanyl, Tramadol, Methadone, Oxycodone

    3. Adjuvant Analgesics:

    Used in combination for neuropathic pain (e.g., certain antidepressants or anticonvulsants with analgesic properties).

Screening and Quality Control of Synthetic Painkillers

    Screening of synthetic painkillers is critical to ensure purity, potency, efficacy, and safety. It involves both qualitative and quantitative analysis using advanced analytical techniques.

    1. Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR):

    Purpose: Identification of functional groups and confirmation of chemical structure. Rapid & non-destructive technique

    Applications: Authentication of raw materials and detection of counterfeit drugs.

    2. High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC):

    Purpose: Quantitative analysis of active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), impurities, and degradation products. High resolution, reproducibility, and sensitivity.

    Applications: Detection of drug purity, batch consistency, and stability testing.

    3. Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS):

    Purpose: Identification and quantification of volatile and semi-volatile compounds. High sensitivity and specificity.

    Applications: Screening of opioids and detecting trace levels of drug metabolites in biological samples (e.g., blood, urine). Purpose: Highly sensitive detection and quantification of compounds in complex matrices.

    4. Liquid Chromatography–Mass Spectrometry (LC-MS/MS):

    Purpose: Highly sensitive detection and quantification of compounds in complex matrices. Combines separation efficiency of LC with detection sensitivity of MS.

    Applications: Bioanalytical testing, pharmacokinetics, and drug metabolism studies.

Importance of Analytical Screening

  • Ensures drug safety and efficacy
  • Detects counterfeit or substandard medicines
  • Supports regulatory compliance
  • Aids in pharmacokinetic and toxicological studies
