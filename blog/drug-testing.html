Antihistamines

    Antihistamines are a class of drugs that block or reduce the effects of histamine, a chemical released by the immune system during allergic reactions. Histamine binds to specific receptors in the body, leading to symptoms like itching, swelling, runny nose, watery eyes, and hives(ulticaria). Antihistamines work by blocking histamine receptors, thus relieving these symptoms.

Types of Antihistamines

    1. H₁-Antihistamines:

    Uses for Allergic conditions such as hay fever, urticaria (hives), allergic rhinitis, conjunctivitis, and insect bites.

    Mechanism: Block histamine at H₁ receptors, which are primarily found in the skin, lungs, and blood vessels.

    Examples:

    First generation drugs: Diphenhydramine, Chlorpheniramine (sedating)

    Second generation drugs: Loratadine, Levocetirizine, Cetirizine, Fexofenadine (non-sedating)

    2. H₂-Antihistamines:

    Used for Gastric conditions such as acid reflux, ulcers, and Zollinger–Ellison syndrome.

    Mechanism: Block histamine at H₂ receptors, found mainly in the stomach lining, reducing gastric acid secretion.

    Examples: Ranitidine, Famotidine, Cimetidine

Key Differences Between First- and Second-Generation H₁-Antihistamines

Feature First-Generation Second-Generation
Sedation High (cross blood-brain barrier) Low or none
Duration of action Short (requires multiple doses/day) Longer-lasting
CNS side effects Common (drowsiness, dizziness) Rare

Bronchodilators

    Bronchodilators are medications used to relax the smooth muscles of the airways (bronchi), leading to dilation (widening) of the air passages in the lungs. This improves airflow and makes breathing easier, especially in conditions like asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and chronic bronchitis.

Types of Bronchodilators

    Based on method of action & duration of action classified into

    1. β2-Adrenergic Agonists (β2-agonists)

    Used to stimulate β2-receptors in bronchial muscles, causing relaxation.

    Examples: Salbutamol (Albuterol), Levalbuterol, Salmeterol, Formoterol, Vilanterol

    2. Anticholinergics (Muscarinic Antagonists)

    They block acetylcholine at muscarinic receptors, preventing bronchoconstriction.

    Examples: Ipratropium bromide, Tiotropium, Aclidinium

    3. Methylxanthines:

    These are phosphodiesterase inhibitors that increase cAMP, causing bronchodilation. Second line drugs for asthma or COPD condition.

    Examples: Aminophylline, Theophylline
